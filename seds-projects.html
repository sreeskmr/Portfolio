NASA Big Ideas Challenge 2022 — Rover Development

As part of SEDS-UCF, I engineered the mechanical body of a small rover for the NASA Big Ideas Challenge. My focus was a chassis that balanced maneuverability, stability, and manufacturability. I modeled the full assembly in SolidWorks, produced dimensioned drawings for the machine shop, and iterated geometry around wheelbase, C.G., and ground clearance to improve obstacle handling. I also maintained the bill of materials and fabrication notes to keep the team unblocked during build.

  • My contributions: chassis design, packaging layout, fastener strategy, and technical drawings
  • Key tools: SolidWorks, technical drawing standards, hand tools, 3D printing for fit checks
  • Outcomes: lightweight frame with improved turning radius and serviceable subassemblies

Liquid Bi-Propellant Rocket Team — Propulsion FMEA and Integration Support

I supported the SEDS/AIAA liquid bi-propellant rocket propulsion team by developing a Failure Modes and Effects Analysis (FMEA) for the propulsion system. I mapped component-level risks to system effects, then proposed mitigations tied to procedures and hardware changes. I also assisted with documentation for ground operations, labeling, and verification steps that improve safety and repeatability during testing.

  • My contributions: propulsion FMEA, operating checklists, hazard controls, interface notes for integration
  • Key tools: FMEA matrices, requirements trace, procedure drafting, version control for documents
  • Outcomes: clearer acceptance criteria and test-readiness gates for propulsion operations

Rapid Prototyping and Lab Support

Beyond design and documentation, I handled quick-turn tasks that kept teams moving. I adapted a consumer garage-door opener for low-voltage control, performed small fabrication jobs, and helped new members learn safe tool use and basic electronics. These contributions reduced bottlenecks and shortened the path from CAD to test.

  • Highlights: low-voltage retrofit with soldering and wiring, 3D-printed brackets, fasteners and hardware kitting
  • Impact: faster iteration cycles and fewer shop rework loops

What I Learned

  • Translate requirements into measurable design targets and testable procedures
  • Design for assembly and serviceability to reduce build time and errors
  • Use FMEA early to prevent expensive fixes later
  • Keep documentation tight so new contributors can plug in quickly
